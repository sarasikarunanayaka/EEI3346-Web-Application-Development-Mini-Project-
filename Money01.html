Old Banknotes.

My favorite thing is to collect old Banknotes. I started collecting this in 2015. I have some banknotes of different periods, of different values, of different colors. Let's take a look at some of them!

100 Rupees Banknote of the Central Bank of Ceylon.

The front of the 100 rupee banknote from the Central Bank of Ceylon with the date 1977 features the national crest of Sri Lanka, which is a lion wielding a sword. There is a view of the historic Polonnaruwa Vatadage on the back side.

50 Rupees Banknote of the Central Bank of Ceylon.

S. W. R. D. Bandaranaike, Ceylon's fourth prime minister, is shown on the old 50 rupee currency. The Rs. 50 bill's reverse has a depiction of a tea plantation and tea pluckers.

20 Rupees Banknote of the Central Bank of Ceylon.

A langur monkey, wood pigeon, dalawa medilla snake, rhino-horned lizard, and a Ceylon blue magpie bird are among the native creatures from Sri Lanka that are depicted on the 20 Ceylonese Rupee notes from the Fauna and Flora series. The Rs.20 banknote additionally features native plant species such a "Batapath-damba" tree and a tree fern.

10 Rupees Banknote of the Central Bank of Ceylon.

The Paththirippuwa, commonly known as the Octogon, is seen on the front of this Sri Lankan 10 rupee banknote in Kandy's Sri Dalada Maligawa (the Temple of the Holy Tooth Relic). Another temple, the sanctuary of Raja Maha Vihara in Tissamaharama, is depicted on the reverse.

5 Rupees Banknote of the Central Bank of Ceylon.

The round stupa structure from Sri Lanka's historic Polonnaruwa Vatadage complex is depicted on the orange-red five Sri Lankan rupee banknote.

2 Rupees Banknote of the Central Bank of Ceylon.

The statue of King Parakramabahu I, who ruled the Sinhalese kingdom of Polonnaruwa from the 11th century to 1310 CE, is depicted on these 2 rupee notes from the Central Bank of Ceylon. An image of the circular Buddhist shrine of Medirigiriya Vatadag can be seen on the reverse of the old two Ceylonese rupee bills.

1 Rupees Banknote of the Central Bank of Ceylon.

The Armorial Ensign of Ceylon with the lion clutching a sword is seen on the Ceylonese one rupee banknote. One of the former capital cities of Sri Lanka, Polonnaruwa, is depicted on the reverse of the 1 rupee bill as a staircase.
