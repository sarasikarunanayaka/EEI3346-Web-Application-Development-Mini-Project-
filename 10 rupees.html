Description.

This 10 Sri Lankan Rupee banknote was one of 12 distinct denominations of Sri Lankan Rupee notes released by the Central Bank of Sri Lanka (Temple of the Tooth). These belong to the series of discontinued Sri Lankan rupee banknotes. These 10 Sri Lankan Rupee banknotes were first printed by the Central Bank of Sri Lanka in 1982. They were withdrawn from circulation in 1991.

The Paththirippuwa, commonly known as the Octogon, is seen on the front of this Sri Lankan 10 rupee banknote in Kandy's Sri Dalada Maligawa (the Temple of the Holy Tooth Relic). Another temple, the sanctuary of Raja Maha Vihara in Tissamaharama, is depicted on the reverse.
