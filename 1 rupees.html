Description.

This 1 rupee Central Bank of Ceylon banknote was one of 12 distinct denominations of Sri Lankan Rupee notes released by the Central Bank of Sri Lanka (Armorial Ensign series). These belong to the series of discontinued Sri Lankan rupee banknotes. These one rupee banknotes were first printed by the Central Bank of Sri Lanka in 1956. In 1962, they were taken out of circulation.

The Armorial Ensign of Ceylon with the lion clutching a sword is seen on the Ceylonese one rupee banknote. One of the former capital cities of Sri Lanka, Polonnaruwa, is depicted on the reverse of the 1 rupee bill as a staircase.
