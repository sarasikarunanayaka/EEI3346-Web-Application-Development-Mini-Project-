2 Rupees Banknote of the Central Bank of Ceylon.

Information.

  • Currency: Sri Lankan Rupees
  • Dimensions: 114 × 59 mm
  • Material: paper
  • Colour: brown, lilac
  • Person: King Parakramabahu the Great, Pali Mahā Parākaramabāhu
  • Profession: king of the Kingdom of Polonnaruwa
  • Text: 2, Central Bank of Ceylon, Two Rupees
  • Place: Polonnaruwa
  • Object: circular Buddhist shrine, flowers, hill, pillars, ruins, statue of King Parakramabahu I
  • Landmark: Medirigiriya Vatadag

Description.

This 2 rupee Central Bank of Ceylon banknote was one of 12 distinct denominations of Sri Lankan Rupee notes released by the Central Bank of Sri Lanka (King Parakramabahu I statue). These belong to the series of discontinued Sri Lankan rupee banknotes. These two Sri Lankan rupee banknotes were first printed in 1969 by the Central Bank of Sri Lanka. They were taken off the market in 1977.

This 2 rupee note from the Central Bank of Ceylon features a picture of the statue of King Parakramabahu I, who governed the Sinhalese kingdom of Polonnaruwa from the 11th century to 1310 CE.
